My Story


I grew up in Northwest Louisiana, and after graduating high school, I enlisted in the United States Navy. I spent five years as an aviation electrician (AE) working on aircraft and the electrical components within them.

After being honorably discharged from the Navy, I spent some time traveling abroad, which I feel gave me a very different look on life. I also began to study accounting while playing in some different bands and exploring the world of music, which led me to pursure a degree in audio production.

While attending the Los Angeles School of Recording, I was also working with artists around the city that had little or no budget so that I could gain experience. After graduation, I was set to start working at a recording studio that I had interned at the previous summer, but that came to a halt due to the covid outbreak.

After moving home to be closer to family during the pandemic, I began to reassess and think about what I would pursue next. While trying to find my footing, I took the opportunity to do something else I'd always wanted to try. The following summer, I went to Alaska and worked on a salmon tender during the salmon fishing season.

Once I returned home, I started exploring new career paths and that led me to software development. I loved the idea of a challenge, as well as it being a way to creatively solve problems. From there I found LEARN Academy, a school based in San Diego, Ca, and I enrolled.
